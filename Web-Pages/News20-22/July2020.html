Pandemic chronology[edit]

1 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 163:[1]

  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reporting 149 new cases the lowest daily case count since June 26 2020.[2][3]
  • Fiji reported the death of one of its nationals who was traveling aboard a repatriation flight operated by Garuda Indonesia.[4][5]
  • Malaysia has reported only one imported case, bringing the number of active cases to 144, with a total of 8,640 cases. 21 patients have recovered, bringing the total to 8,375. The death toll remains at 121.[6]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, keeping the number of active cases at 22, with a total number of 1,528 cases (1,178 confirmed and 350 probable), 1,484 recoveries and 22 deaths. One case remains in hospital.[7]
  • Singapore has reported 215 new cases, bringing the total to 44,122.[8][9]
  • Ukraine has reported 664 new cases and 14 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 44,998 and 1,173 respectively; a total of 19,548 patients have recovered.[10]

2 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 164:[11]

  • Brazil has reported 1,038 new deaths, taking the country's total to 60,632.[12][13]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 153 new cases.[2][14]
  • France has reported 14 new deaths, taking the total to 29,875. The number of hospitalised cases decreases by 188 to 8,148, and the total number of people in intensive care fell by 9 to 573.[12]
  • Malaysia has reported three new cases, bringing the total number to 8,643 cases with 85 active cases. 62 have recovered, bringing the number of recovered to 8,437. The death toll remains at 121.[15]
  • New Zealand has reported two new cases, bringing the total number to 1,530 cases (1,180 confirmed and 350 probable). Six people have recovered, bringing the number of active cases down to 18 and the total number of recoveries to 1,490. The death toll remains at 22.[16]
  • Serbia reported 359 new cases and 6 new deaths, bringing the totals to 15,195 and 287 respectively. There are currently 1,996 active cases with 81 patients in intensive care.[17]
  • Singapore has reported 188 new cases, bringing the total to 44,310. 418 have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 39,429.[18]
  • Ukraine has reported 889 new cases and 12 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 45,887 and 1,185 respectively; a total of 20,053 patients have recovered.[19]
  • The United Kingdom reported 89 new deaths, taking the country's official total to 43,995, although estimates place the true number closer to 55,000.[12][20][21]
  • The United States has reported 50,700 new cases, the highest daily number of new infections recorded so far. The total is over 2.73 million.[12][22]

3 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 165:[23]

  • Brazil has reported 37,923 new cases, bringing the total to over 1.5 million. A further 1,091 deaths were recorded, bringing that total to 64,265.[24][25]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 165 new cases.[26][27]
  • France has reported 18 new deaths, taking the total to 29,893. It was also reported that the number of people in intensive care fell by 13 to 560.[28]
  • India has reported a daily record of 20,903 new cases, bringing the country's total to 625,544. 379 deaths were also recorded, taking the total death toll to 18,213.[28]
  • Iran has reported 154 new deaths, taking the country's total to 11,260. The total number of cases was confirmed to stand at 235,429.[28]
  • Japan reported 124 new cases in the capital Tokyo, with 70 per cent of the infected below the age of forty.[29]
  • Malaysia has reported five new cases (three imported and two community transmissions), with the number of active cases being 81 and the total number being 8,648 cases. Nine people have recovered, bringing the number of recovered to 8,446. The death toll remains at 121.[30]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the number of active cases remaining 18 and the total number remaining 1,530 (1,180 confirmed and 350 probable). There have been a total of 1,490 recoveries and 22 deaths.[31]
  • Saudi Arabia has reported 4,193 new cases, taking the country's total past 200,000 to 201,801. 1,802 deaths were also reported, with over 140,000 having recovered from the virus.[28][32]
  • Singapore has reported 169 new cases, bringing the total to 44,479. 340 have recovered, bringing the total number of recoveries to 39,769. The death toll remains at 26.[33]
  • Spain has reported 17 new deaths in the last 24 hours.[28][34]
  • South Korea has reported 63 new cases, bringing the total to 12,967. The death toll remained at 282.[28]
  • Ukraine has reported 876 new cases and 27 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 46,763 and 1,212 respectively; a total of 20,558 patients have recovered.[35]
  • The United Kingdom has reported 137 new deaths, bringing the confirmed total to 44,131, with a 544 people testing positive for coronavirus.[28][36]
  • The United States reported 57,683 new cases, the highest daily increase so far, bringing the total number of cases to 2,793,022. A further 728 deaths were also reported, bringing the death toll to 129,405.[28][37]

4 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 166:[38]

  • Australia has reported 108 new cases in the state of Victoria, the biggest daily increase since March.[39][40]
  • Azerbaijan has reported 534 new cases, bringing the total to 19,801. The death toll rose to 241 and the number of recoveries was reported to be at almost 11,300.[24]
  • Brazil has recorded 37,923 new cases.[41]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 121 new cases the lowest daily case count since June 26 2020.[42][43]
  • India has reported a new daily record of 22,772 cases, bringing the total to 648,315. A further 442 deaths were reported, bringing the total death toll thus far to 18,655.[24][44]
  • Malaysia has reported ten new cases, bringing the total to 8,658. There are 76 active cases, with two requiring respirator support. 15 patients have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,461. The death toll remains at 121.[45]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the number of active cases at 18 and the total number at 1,530 cases (1,180 confirmed and 350 probable). There have been a total of 1,490 recoveries and 22 deaths.[46]
  • Russia has reported 6,632 new cases and 168 new deaths, bringing the totals to 674,515 and 10,027 respectively.[24]
  • Singapore has reported 185 new cases, bringing the total to 44,664.[47][48]
  • South Africa has reported 9,064 new cases, the highest daily total so far, with more than 177,000 cases now confirmed in the country.[24]
  • South Korea has reported 63 new cases, taking the total to 13,030. The death toll remained at 283.[24]
  • Ukraine has reported 914 new cases and 15 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 47,677 and 1,227 respectively; a total of 21,155 patients have recovered.[49]

5 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 167:[50]

  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 138 new cases.[51][52]
  • Malaysia has reported five new cases, with the number of active cases at 77 and the total number at 8,663. Four patients have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,465. The death toll remains at 121.[53]
  • New Zealand has reported three new cases, with the number of active cases at 21 and the total number at 1,533 cases (1,183 confirmed and 340 probable). There have been a total of 1,490 recoveries and 22 deaths.[54]
  • Singapore has reported 136 new cases, bringing the total to 44,800.[55] 324 have been discharged as of Sunday, bringing the total number of recoveries to 40,441.[56]
  • Ukraine has reported 823 new cases and 22 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 48,500 and 1,249 respectively; a total of 21,376 patients have recovered.[57]

6 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 168:[58]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 127 cases, the biggest overnight spike in Australia since the pandemic began. Australia also recorded two deaths, bringing the death toll to 106.[59][60]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 154 new cases.[61][62]
  • Fiji reported the country's first case in 78 days in a tourist returning from India.[63]
  • France reported 27 new deaths, taking the total to 29,920. The number of patients in intensive care was recorded to have fallen by 12 to 548.[64]
  • India reported 24,428 new cases, bringing the country's total to 697,413.[64][65]
  • Indonesia reported 1,209 new cases and 70 new deaths, bringing the totals to 64,958 and 3,241 respectively.[64]
  • Malaysia has reported five new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 77 and the total number to 8,668. 11 cases have been discharged, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,476.[66]
  • New Zealand has reported one new case, bringing the number of active cases to 22 and the total number to 1,534 cases (1,184 confirmed and 350 probable). There have been a total of 1,490 recoveries and 22 deaths.[67]
  • Pakistan reported 3,344 new cases, including Health Secretary Zafar Mirza, bringing the total to 231,818. 50 new deaths were also reported, bringing that total to 4,844.[64][68]
  • Qatar reported 546 new cases and 5 new deaths, bringing the totals to 100,345 and 133 respectively.[69][64]
  • Singapore has reported 183 new cases, bringing the total to 44,983.[70][71]
  • Ukraine has reported 543 new cases and 13 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 49,043 and 1,262 respectively; a total of 21,703 patients have recovered.[72]
  • The United Kingdom reported 16 new deaths, the lowest figure since 16 March, bringing the total to 44,236. No new deaths were reported in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland.[64][73]
  • The United States passed 130,000 deaths to record a total of 130,007. It was also reported that the total number of cases had reached 2,888,729.[64][74]
  • Vietnam reported 14 imported cases, bringing the country's total number of cases to 369. No further deaths have been reported, with 90% of cases having recovered.[64]

7 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 169:[75]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 191 new cases.[76]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 112 new cases the lowest daily case count since June 26 2020.[77][78]
  • Fiji has confirmed two new border quarantine cases.[79]
  • France reported 13 new deaths, taking the total to 29,933. This figure accounts for a downward revision of the number of nursing home fatalities; the number who died in the country's hospitals increased by 34 to 19,457. The number of fatalities in nursing homes was confirmed to be 10,476, a decrease from the figure of 10,497 reported a week earlier.[80]
  • Germany reported 397 new cases and 12 new deaths, bringing the totals to 197,341 and 9,036 respectively, according to data from the Robert Koch Institute.[81][82]
  • India reported 22,252 new cases and 467 new deaths, taking the death toll above 20,000 to 20,160.[83]
  • Indonesia reported 1,268 new cases and 68 new deaths, bringing the totals to 66,226 and 3,309 respectively. It was also reported that 30,785 people have recovered so far.[80]
  • Iran reported 2,637 new cases, taking the total number of positive cases to 245,688. The country also reported a record daily increase of 200 deaths, bringing the total to 11,931.[80]
  • Malaysia has reported six new cases, with the number of active cases at 72 and the total number at 8,674. Five have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,481. The death tolls remains at 121.[84]
  • New Zealand has reported two new cases and two new recoveries, bringing the number of active cases to 22 and the number of recovered to 1,492. There have been a total of 1,536 cases (1,186 confirmed and 350 probable). The death toll has been 22.[85]
  • Serbia reported 299 new cases and 13 deaths, bringing the totals to 16,168 and 330 respectively. 110 patients were reported to be in intensive care.[80][86]
  • Singapore has reported 157 new cases, bringing the total to 45,140.[87][88]
  • South Africa reported 8,971 cases, taking the total over 200,000 to 205,721.[80]
  • Russia reported 6,368 new cases and 198 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 694,230 and 10,494 respectively.[80]
  • Ukraine has reported 564 new cases and 21 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 49,607 and 1,283 respectively; a total of 22,139 patients have recovered.[89]
  • The United Kingdom reported 581 new cases, taking the country's total to 286,349. A further 155 deaths were also recorded, bringing the official total to 44,391, although the Office for National Statistics puts the number of excess deaths from COVID-19 over 55,000.[80][90][91]
  • The United States reported that the total number of cases has passed 3 million.[81][92][93]

8 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 170:[94]

  • Argentina reported a daily record of 3,604 new cases, raising the total number of confirmed cases to 275,003. The death toll was confirmed to be at 1,694.[95][96]
  • Australia reported 134 new cases in the state of Victoria.[81][97]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 118 new cases.[98][99]
  • France reported 32 new deaths, bringing the total to 29,965.[100]
  • Hong Kong reported 24 new cases, 19 of which were confirmed to be locally transmitted. The total number of cases stands at 1,324 with 7 deaths.[81]
  • Iran reported 153 new deaths, bringing the total death toll to over 12,000. It was also reported that the total number of cases has reached 248,379 and that 209,463 have recovered.[81][101]
  • Malaysia has reported three new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 70 and the total number to 8,677. Five patients have been discharged, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,486. The death toll remains at 121.[102]
  • Mexico reported 6,995 new cases and 782 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 275,003 and 32,796 respectively.[95][103]
  • New Zealand has reported one new case, bringing the number of active cases to 23 and the total number to 1,537 (1,187 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered remains 1,492 and the death toll 22.[104] The latest case is a 32-year-old man who briefly escaped managed isolation in Auckland before being detained by the authorities.[105]
  • Oman reported 1,210 new cases, taking the total over 50,000 to 50,207. 9 new deaths were also reported, raising the death toll to 233.[81]
  • The Philippines reported 2,539 new cases, the highest daily increase recorded thus far. A further 5 deaths were also reported, raising the death toll to 1,314.[81][106]
  • Russia reported 6,562 new cases, taking the total number of cases above 700,000 to 700,792. A further 173 deaths were also reported, bringing the total confirmed death toll to 10,667.[81]
  • Singapore has reported 158 new cases, bringing the total to 45,298.[107] In addition, a patient who had COVID-19 died from respiratory arrest.[108]
  • Ukraine has reported 807 new cases and 23 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 50,414 and 1,306 respectively; a total of 23,119 patients have recovered.[109]
  • The United Kingdom reported 630 new cases and 126 new deaths, taking the country's official totals to 286,979 and 44,517 respectively.[110]

9 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 171:[111]

  • Bolivian President Jeanine Áñez tested positive.[95]
  • China reported 9 new cases, all of which were imported, raising the total number of cases to 84,910. No further deaths were reported, leaving the death toll at 4,641. 74 people remain hospitalised.[95]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 170 new cases the highest daily case count since June 29 2020.[112][113]
  • Germany reported 442 new cases and 12 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 197,783 and 9,048 respectively, according to data from the Robert Koch Institute.[95]
  • Hong Kong reported 42 new cases, 34 of which were confirmed to be domestically transmitted, raising the total number of cases thus far to 1,366. No further deaths were reported, leaving the death toll at 7.[95][114]
  • India reported a daily record of 24,879 newly confirmed cases, raising the total to 767,296 cases. A further 487 deaths were also recorded, bringing the total death toll to 21,129.[95]
  • Indonesia reported 2,657 new cases and 58 new deaths, bringing the totals to 70,736 and 3,417 respectively.[95]
  • Japan reported 224 new cases in the capital Tokyo, the largest daily increase so far.[115]
  • Malaysia has reported six new cases, bringing the total number to 8,683. 13 cases have been discharged, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,499. There are 63 active cases, with two in intensive care and one on ventilator support. The death toll remains at 121.[116]
  • Mexico reported 7,280 new cases, the highest daily total so far, and 730 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 282,283 and 33,526 respectively.[117]
  • New Zealand has reported three new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 24 and the total number to 1,540 (1,190 confirmed 350 probable). There were two reported new recoveries, bringing the total number of recovered to 1,494. The death toll remains at 22.[118]
  • Nigeria reported 460 new cases, taking the country's total above 30,000 to 30,249. The total death toll was reported to be at 684.[95]
  • Qatar reported 557 new cases and 4 new deaths, bringing the totals to 102,110 and 142 respectively.[119]
  • Singapore has reported 125 new cases, bringing the total to 45,422.[120] In addition, a patient who had COVID-19 died from respiratory arrest.[121]
  • Slovakia reported 53 new cases, the biggest increase since April, raising the total to 1,851. It was also reported that the death toll stands at 28, with 1,477 recoveries. The number of active cases was confirmed to be 346, the highest since May 12.[95][122]
  • South Africa reported 13,674 new cases, the highest daily total so far, raising the total number of confirmed cases so far to 238,339. A further 129 deaths were recorded, bringing the total death toll to 3,720; 113,061 patients have recovered. The health department also announced that over two million tests had now been performed.[117][123]
  • South Korea reported 50 new cases, raising the total to 13,293. The death toll remained at 287.[95]
  • Ukraine has reported 810 new cases and 21 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 51,224 and 1,327 respectively; a total of 23,784 patients have recovered.[124]
  • The United Kingdom reported 85 new deaths, bringing the total death toll to 44,602. No new deaths were reported in Scotland.[95][125]
  • The United States reported 65,551 new cases, the highest daily amount so far, raising the total number of confirmed cases to over 3.1 million. According to data from Johns Hopkins University, 1,000 deaths were recorded in the last 24 hours, raising the country's death toll to 133,195.[126][117]

10 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 172:[127]

  • The World Health Organization reported a record increase in global coronavirus cases, with the total rising by 228,102 in 24 hours.[117]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 116 new cases.[128][129]
  • The Czech Republic reported 82 new cases, taking the total over 13,000.[117]
  • Fiji has confirmed five news cases.[130]
  • France reported over 600 new cases for the third consecutive day. 25 new deaths were recorded, taking the total death toll over 30,000 to 30,004.[117]
  • Hong Kong reported 38 new cases, raising the total confirmed so far to 1,404. The death toll remains at 7.[131][117]
  • India reported 26,506 new cases, the highest daily number so far.[117]
  • Indonesia reported 1,611 new cases and 52 new deaths, bringing the numbers to 72,347 and 3,469 respectively. It was also confirmed that 33,529 patients have recovered.[117]
  • Lebanon reported 71 new cases, the highest daily number so far.[117]
  • Malaysia has reported 13 new cases, bringing the total number of cases to 8,696.[132] 12 patients have been discharged, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,511. There are 64 active cases while the death toll remains at 121.[133]
  • Namibia reported 52 new cases and its first death, a 45-year-old man. The total number of cases has reached 667.[134]
  • New Zealand has reported two new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 23 and the total number to 1,542 (1,192 confirmed and 350 probable). Three new cases were reported, bringing the total number of recovered to 1,497. The death toll remains at 22.[135]
  • Serbia reported 386 new cases and 18 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 386 and 18 respectively.[136]
  • Russia reported 6,635 new cases and 174 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 713,936 and 11,017 respectively, with 489,068 patients having recovered.[117][137]
  • Singapore has reported 191 new cases, bringing the total to 45,613.[138][139]
  • Ukraine has reported 819 new cases and 18 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 52,043 and 1,345 respectively; a total of 24,800 patients have recovered.[140]
  • The United Kingdom reported 48 new deaths, taking the official total to 44,650. No new deaths were recorded in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland.[117][141]
  • The United States reported 69,000 new cases, another daily record, raising the total to 3.18 million, with more than 134,000 deaths confirmed so far.[142][143]

11 July[edit]

WHO Situation Report 173:[144]

  • Australia reported 216 new cases in the state of Victoria.[145]
  • Brazil reported 45,000 new cases, taking the total to 1.8 million. 1,200 new deaths were also recorded, taking the death toll over 70,000 to 70,400.[145][146]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario is reporting 130 new cases.[147][148]
  • India reported a new high (for the third consecutive day) of 27,114 daily confirmed cases, taking the total over 800,000 to 820,916. 519 new deaths were also reported, raising that total to 22,123.[145][149]
  • Italy reported 188 new cases, a third of which were in the region of Lombardy, bringing the total to 242,827. 7 new deaths were also reported, raising the total to 34,945. The total number of active cases fell to 13,303; over 5.95 million tests have been performed.[150][151]
  • Lebanon reported a daily record of 86 new cases, raising the country's total death toll to 2,168. A total of 36 deaths have been reported.[145]
  • Malaysia has reported eight new cases (four imported cases), bringing the total number to 8,704. There are 67 active cases, with three in intensive care and two on ventilator support. Four patients have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,515. The country also reported one new death, bringing the total to 122.[152]
  • Mexico reported 6,094 new cases and 539 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 295,268 and 34,730 respectively.[150]
  • New Zealand has reported one new case, bringing the number of active cases to 24 and the total number to 1,543 (1,193 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered remains at 1,497 while the death toll remains at 24.[153]
  • Palestine recorded 436 new cases, bringing the total number confirmed thus far to 5,827. A further 5 deaths were recorded, raising the total death toll to 35. 829 people have recovered.[145]
  • The Philippines reported 1,387 new cases, raising the total to 54,222. 12 new deaths were also recorded.[145][154]
  • Russia reported 6,611 new cases and 188 new deaths, bringing the total numbers so far to 720,547 and 11,205 respectively.[145]
  • Singapore has reported 170 new cases, bringing the total to 45,783.[155][156]
  • South Africa reported 13,497 new cases and 3,971 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 264,184 and 3,971 respectively.[157]
  • Turkey reported 1,016 new cases and 21 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 211,981 and 5,344 respectively. It was also announced that 1,334 patients have recovered, bringing the total number of recoveries to 193,217.[145]
  • Ukraine has reported 800 new cases and 27 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 52,843 and 1,372 respectively; a total of 25,661 patients have recovered.[158]
  • The United Kingdom reported 820 new cases and 148 new deaths, taking the official totals to 288,953 and 44,798 respectively. It was also announced that 153,667 tests had been performed in the last 24 hours.[159][160]
  • The United States reported 66,528 new cases and 760 new deaths, raising the totals to 3,242,073 and 134,729 respectively, according to data from Johns Hopkins University. Florida reported the largest daily increase in cases in a single state so far, with 15,299 positive cases confirmed.[150][161]

12 July[edit]
