Pandemic chronology[edit]

1 September[edit]

  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 656 new cases.[1][2][3]
  • Fiji has confirmed 290 new cases, bringing the total number to 47,006. Eight new deaths were reported, bringing the death toll to 504. There are 17,124 active cases.[4]
  • Iran surpasses 5 million cases.[5]
  • Malaysia has reported 18,762 new cases, bringing the total number to 1,765,016. There are 21,073 recoveries, bringing the total number of recoveries to 1,482,800. There are 278 deaths, bringing the death toll to 16,942. There are 265,274 active cases, with 1,007 in intensive care and 464 on ventilator support.[6]
  • New Zealand has reported 77 new cases while one previously reported case was reclassified, bringing the total number to 3,645 (3,288 confirmed and 357 probable). There are three recoveries, bringing the total number of recoveries to 2,895. The death toll remains 26. There are 724 active cases (682 in the community and 42 in managed isolation).[7]
  • The Philippines surpasses 2 million cases.[8]
  • Singapore has reported 180 new cases including 177 locally transmitted and three imported, bringing the total to 67,800. Of the locally transmitted cases, 90 of them are unlinked.[9] The country has vaccinated a total of 4,515,469 people, with 4,328,804 being fully vaccinated as of yesterday. The death toll remains at 55.[10]
  • Ukraine has reported 2,075 new daily cases and 44 new daily deaths, bringing the total number to 2,288,371 and 53,833 respectively; a total of 2,208,865 patients have recovered.[11]
  • American musician Gene Simmons of Kiss has tested positive for COVID-19. As a result, the band has postponed several upcoming concert shows.[12]
  • Boston Red Sox baseball shortstop Xander Bogaerts has tested positive for COVID-19 and was pulled out of the baseball game against Tampa Bay Rays.[13]
