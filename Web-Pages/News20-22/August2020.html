Pandemic chronology[edit]

1 August[edit]

WHO Situation Report 194:[1]

  • Australia has reported three new deaths (two in Victoria and one in New South Wales), bringing the death toll to 201. The state of Victoria has reported 397 new cases.[2]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 124 new cases.[3][4]
  • Malaysia has reported nine new cases, bringing the total number of cases to 8,985. There are 213 active cases, with two in intensive care and one of these on ventilator support. Three people have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,647. The death toll remains at 125.[5]
  • Mexico has reported a total of 46,688 deaths and 424,637 infections, giving the country the third highest death rate from COVID-19.[6]
  • New Zealand has reported two new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 22 and the total number to 1,562 (1,212 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered remains at 1,518 while the death toll remains at 22.[7]
  • Singapore has reported 307 new cases, bringing the total to 52,512.[8][9]
  • Ukraine has reported 1,172 new cases and 16 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 71,056 and 1,709 respectively; a total of 39,308 patients have recovered.[10]

2 August[edit]

WHO Situation Report 195:[11]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 671 new cases and seven deaths within the last 24 hours.[12][13]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 116 new cases.[14][15]
  • Malaysia has reported 14 new cases, bringing the total number to 8,999. 17 people recovered, bringing the total number of recoveries to 8,664. The death toll stands at 125.[16]
  • New Zealand has reported three new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 25 and the total number to 1,565 (1,215 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered remains at 1,518 and the death toll stands at 22.[17]
  • Singapore has reported 313 new cases, bringing the total to 52,825.[18][19]
  • Ukraine has reported 1,112 new cases and 16 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 72,168 and 1,725 respectively; a total of 39,543 patients have recovered.[20]

3 August[edit]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 429 new cases and 13 deaths, bringing the total number of active cases in Victoria to 6,489.[22]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 88 new cases.[23][24]
  • Malaysia has reported two new cases, bringing the total number to 9,001. Four patients were discharged, bringing the number of recovered to 8,670. There are 208 active cases while the death toll stands at 125.[25]
  • New Zealand has reported two new cases in managed isolation, bringing the total number of active cases to 27 and the total number to 1,567 (1,217 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered remains at 1,518 and the death toll stands at 22.[26]
  • Singapore has reported 226 new cases, bringing the total to 53,051.[27][28]
  • Ukraine has reported 990 new cases and 13 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 73,158 and 1,738 respectively; a total of 39,876 patients have recovered.[29]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 439 new cases and 11 deaths.[31]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 91 new cases.[32][33]
  • Malaysia has reported one new case, bringing the total number to 9,002. There are 193 active cases and a total of 8,684 recoveries.[34]
  • New Zealand has reported five new recoveries, bringing the total number of recovered to 1,523. There are 22 active case, with the total number of cases remaining 1,567 (1,217 confirmed and 350 probable) and the death toll at 22.[35]
  • Singapore has reported 295 new cases, bringing the total to 53,346.[36][37]
  • Ukraine has reported 1,061 new cases and 26 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 74,219 and 1,764 respectively; a total of 40,613 patients have recovered.[38]

5 August[edit]

WHO Situation Report 198:[39]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 725 new cases, bringing the total to 13,035. 15 new deaths were reported, bringing the state's death toll to 162.[40]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 86 new cases.[41][42]
  • Malaysia has reported 21 new cases, bringing the total number to 9,023. 18 have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,702. [43]
  • New Zealand has two new cases, bringing the number of active cases to 24 and the total number to 1,569 (1,219 confirmed and 350 probable). The number of recovered and death remains 1,523 and 22 respectively.[44]
  • Singapore has reported 908 new cases, bringing the total to 54,254.[45][46]
  • Ukraine has reported record high 1,271 new daily cases as well as 24 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 75,490 and 1,788 respectively; a total of 41,527 patients have recovered.[47]

6 August[edit]

WHO Situation Report 199:[48]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 471 new cases and eight new deaths, bringing the death toll to 170. In addition, 107 "mystery cases" had emerged while 1,533 active cases were linked to aged care facilities.[49]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 95 new cases.[50][51]
  • Malaysia has reported 15 new cases, bringing the total number to 9,038. 11 have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,713. There are 200 active cases while the death toll remains at 125.[52]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the total number of cases remaining 1,569 (1,219 confirmed and 350 probable cases). One new recovery was reported, bringing the total number to 1,524. There are 23 active cases while the death toll remains 22.[53]
  • Singapore has reported 301 new cases, bringing the total to 54,555.[54][55]
  • Ukraine has reported record high 1,318 new daily cases and repeated the previously set record of 31 new daily deaths, bringing the total numbers to 76,808 and 1,819 respectively; a total of 42,524 patients have recovered.[56]

7 August[edit]

WHO Situation Report 200:[57]

  • The Australian state of Victoria has reported 450 new cases, bringing the state's total to 13,867. The infected include 911 health workers.[58]
  • India has reported 2,027,000 confirmed cases, including 41,585 deaths.[59]
  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reported 88 new cases.[60][61]
  • Malaysia has reported 25 new cases, bringing the total number to 9,063. 15 have recovered, bring the total number of recoveries to 8,728. The death tolls remains at 125.[62]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the number of active cases remaining 23 and the total number of cases remaining 1,569 (1,219 confirmed and 350 probable cases). The number of recovered remains 1,524 and the death toll remains 22.[63]
  • Singapore has reported 242 new cases, bringing the total to 54,797.[64] In addition, the Inter-agency Taskforce has completed testing in all foreign worker dormitories except for a few standalone blocks serving as quarantine facilities.[65]
  • Ukraine has reported record high 1,453 new daily cases as well as record high 33 new daily deaths, bringing the total numbers to 78,261 and 1,852 respectively; a total of 43,055 patients have recovered.[66]

  • Malaysia has reported seven new cases, bringing the total to 9,070. 47 cases have recovered, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,775. There are 170 active cases, with two in intensive care and one of them on ventilator support. The death toll remains 125.[71]
  • New Zealand has reported no new cases, with the number of active cases remaining 23 and the total number of cases remaining 1,569 (1,219 confirmed and 350 probable cases). The number of recovered remains 1,524 and the death toll remains 22.[72]
  • Singapore has reported 132 new cases, bringing the total to 54,929.[73][74]
  • Ukraine has reported record high 1,489 new daily cases as well as 27 new daily deaths, bringing the total numbers to 79,750 and 1,879 respectively; a total of 43,655 patients have recovered.[75]

WHO Situation Report 204:[96]

  • The Canadian province of Ontario has reporting 33 new cases the lowest daily case count since March 18 2020.[97][98]
  • Malaysia has reported nine new cases, bringing the total to 9,103. Six patients were discharged, bringing the total number of recovered to 8,809. The death toll remains at 125.[99]

The number of recovered remains 1,526 while the death toll remains 22.[100] Later that day, four cases of community transmissions were reported, the first cases outside of a managed isolation facility for 102 days.[101]

  • Ukraine has reported 1,158 new cases and 29 new deaths, bringing the total numbers to 83,115 and 1,951 respectively; a total of 44,934 patients have recovered.[103]
